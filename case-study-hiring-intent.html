GTM tooling (Smartlead, Clay) Nearshoring Use Case — US Tech Market

Hiring Signal-Based Account Discovery

I built a Clay workflow for a nearshoring company to identify US tech companies actively hiring software engineers and qualify them for outbound.

Most nearshoring agencies spray and pray toward any US tech company. I engineered a system that identifies active hiring signals and distributed team patterns to find the 1% of companies ready to hire now.

1. Score The Signals

I don't just find a company name; I map their technical DNA and hiring friction.

I analyze:

  • Live Hiring Activity: Software Engineer and Software Developer roles as the primary intent trigger.
  • Velocity Flags: If the company is hiring multiple engineering roles and how many.
  • Funding Momentum: Checks if the company raised capital in the last 12 months.
  • Tech Stack Keywords: Detecting specific environments like React, Node, .NET, Java, Python, and AWS.

Because if a company is funded and hiring 5+ devs in a specific stack, they aren't just "growing"—they are hit with a recruitment bottleneck that nearshoring solves.

2. Map The "Offshore DNA"

People say, "target the market."
But I map the readiness.

I'm not trying to find every tech company; I'm filtering for those already comfortable with remote, global talent.

My system detects existing distributed teams in:

  • Latin America (LATAM)
  • Southeast Asia (SEA)
  • Eastern Europe

I also execute a Global Purge: The system automatically excludes staffing firms, recruiting agencies, dev shops, and consulting firms. My task is to expand the high-intent pipeline with 100% ICP purity.

3. Build Systems That Turn Data Into Adaptive Outbound

If your email is static, you're an operator. If your email adapts, you're a GTM Engineer.

I designed conditional logic that changes the pitch based on the company's specific situation:

  • Scenario A: High hiring volume + Recent funding = Speed to market pitch.
  • Scenario B: Existing offshore headcount + Specific Tech Stack = Scale and compatibility pitch.
  • Scenario C: First-time offshore + New funding = Risk mitigation and talent density pitch.

The output is a sales-ready Clay engine that identifies the Technical Core (CTO, VP Engineering, or Head of Engineering), enriches their verified LinkedIn/Email, and feeds them into a dynamic outreach machine.

The GTM Engine becomes a system that predicts who will buy before the first email is even sent.

This is why I say GTM Engineering is revenue engineering. If all you can do is "run outbound in Clay," you're an operator. A GTME understands:

  • Revenue patterns
  • Hiring friction
  • Adaptive logic
  • Automation architecture

This is the difference between running a campaign and engineering the entire revenue machine.
