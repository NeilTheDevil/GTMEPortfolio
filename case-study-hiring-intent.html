1% Target: Companies Ready to Buy Now

The Problem

The entire market was targeting everyone.
Nobody was targeting the right moment.

Nearshoring agencies had a data problem disguised as a sales problem. They were sending generic outbound to massive static lists — any US tech company they could find — and wondering why conversion was flat.

The real issue was timing and signal. A company only becomes a fit for nearshoring at a very specific inflection point: when they're actively trying to hire engineers they can't afford or find fast enough. That window is narrow. Miss it and the budget disappears into a local hire.

My task was to build a Clay-powered engine that didn't just find companies — it identified the ones in that exact window, in real time, and routed them into a pitch that matched their specific situation.

The System — 3 Layers

I don't find company names.
I map their technical DNA and hiring friction.

01

Score The Signals

Every company in the workflow is scored against a compound set of intent indicators. I don't use a single trigger — a company has to clear multiple thresholds before it enters the pipeline.

⚡ Live Hiring Activity

Software Engineer and Software Developer roles as the primary intent trigger. If they're actively posting, they're actively failing to fill.

📈 Velocity Flags

How many engineering roles are open simultaneously. A company hiring 5+ devs in the same stack isn't growing — they're hitting a recruitment bottleneck that nearshoring solves.

💰 Funding Momentum

Checks if the company raised capital in the last 12 months. Fresh funding means fresh headcount budget — and pressure to deploy it fast.

🧬 Tech Stack Keywords

Detecting specific environments: React, Node, .NET, Java, Python, AWS. Stack match determines which nearshoring team is the right fit.

If a company is funded and hiring 5+ devs in a specific stack, they aren't just "growing" — they are hit with a recruitment bottleneck that nearshoring solves.

02

Map The "Offshore DNA"

People say "target the market." I map the readiness. Finding a tech company is easy. Finding one already comfortable with remote, global talent — that's a different discipline.

The system scans for existing distributed team patterns across three key regions:

Latin America (LATAM)
Southeast Asia (SEA)
Eastern Europe

Companies with existing offshore headcount have already cleared the biggest psychological hurdle — trust in remote talent. They're exponentially easier to close.

Global Purge

The system automatically excludes staffing firms, recruiting agencies, dev shops, and consulting firms. The goal is 100% ICP purity — high-intent companies, not industry noise.

03

Build Systems That Turn Data Into Adaptive Outbound

If your email is static, you're an operator. If your email adapts, you're a GTM Engineer.

I designed conditional logic that changes the pitch based on each company's specific hiring situation. Three scenarios. Three distinct narratives.

Scenario A
High hiring volume + Recent funding
Speed to market pitch

You raised. You need engineers yesterday. Here's how to deploy 5 devs in 30 days.

Scenario B
Existing offshore headcount + Specific tech stack
Scale and compatibility pitch

You already work with remote teams. Here's a compatible, stack-specific team that plugs in without friction.

Scenario C
First-time offshore + New funding
Risk mitigation and talent density pitch

You've never done this. Here's how to do it with zero risk, full oversight, and the right talent density from day one.

The Output

A sales-ready engine that identifies, enriches, and routes — automatically.

The end state of this workflow is a fully enriched lead record for the Technical Core — the CTO, VP Engineering, or Head of Engineering — with verified LinkedIn profile and email, routed directly into a dynamic outreach sequence.

No SDR manually researching job boards. No guessing on pitch angle. The system already knows the company's situation and has selected the right narrative before a single person opens the email draft.
