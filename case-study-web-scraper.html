Custom Engineering / Claude Code Local Business Intelligence Protocol

High-Fidelity Business Data Enrichment

I engineered a specialized data enrichment protocol for a client who needed to transform raw, messy local business lists into a structured, verified CSV database for high-stakes outbound.

Most data scrapers guess. I built a system that validates. By prioritizing Google Business Profiles and cross-referencing geographic signals, I ensured that the "official website" is never a guess, but a verified fact.

1. The Primary Extraction Strategy

I don't just search for a name; I engineer a search context that forces the most accurate result to the top.

I analyze:

  • GBP Priority: The system treats Google Business Profiles as the "Single Source of Truth."
  • Keyword Injection: Automatically appending business type keywords (Cleaning, SaaS, Medical) to the search string to bypass "Name Twins" in other industries.
  • Geo-Fencing: Locking the search to the specific State and Country to eliminate false positives from different regions.

Because if you are reaching out to a "Blue Sky Cleaning" in Texas, your data engine better not hand you the website for one in London.

2. Controlled Exploration & Disambiguation

People say, "find the link."
But I engineer the Validation Path.

When a clear match isn't immediately available, my system enters a fallback loop across:

  • High-intent directories (Yelp, LinkedIn, Facebook Business).
  • Chamber of Commerce listings.
  • Social signal cross-referencing.

The Disambiguation Logic: If two businesses share a name, the system is hard-coded to reject the lead unless BOTH the location and business type are a 100% match. I prioritize an empty string over a "loose match" because bad data is more expensive than no data.

3. The Multi-Point Validation Protocol

If your data isn't verified, you're an operator. If your data is bulletproof, you're a GTM Engineer.

Before any website is committed to the final output, it must pass a mandatory 4-point check:

  • Name Consistency: Exact or high-confidence variation.
  • Geographic Alignment: City/State consistency check.
  • Category Match: Do the services listed match the input intent?
  • Contact Sync: Do phone numbers/addresses align across multiple sources?

The Result: A structured CSV output with Confidence Scoring (High | Medium | Low | None) and a Reasoning Log, giving the sales team total transparency into why a lead was qualified or rejected.

The GTM Engine becomes a system that ensures your outbound never hits a "dead end."

This is why I say GTM Engineering is revenue engineering. If all you can do is "find a website," you're a scraper. A GTME understands:

  • Data integrity
  • Geographic disambiguation
  • Validation logic
  • Confidence architecture

This is the difference between having a list and having a pipeline.
